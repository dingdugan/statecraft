# Spec: 国家模拟游戏 — working title **"Statecraft / 庙堂"**

> A pure text + numbers nation simulator. No map, no sprites. You are the head of
> government of a **real country** and you make **real national-level decisions** —
> budgets, taxes, policy, crises — then advance the year and watch interlocking
> systems (economy, people, politics) respond. Think *Sid Meier's Civilization*
> with the graphics removed and the governing turned up.

Generated by the LFG brainstorm phase (office-hours method, autonomous/builder mode)
on 2026-06-19. Mode: Builder. Status: ACTIVE (drives the build).

---

## 问题陈述 / Concept

Most "country games" are either (a) wargames with maps, or (b) idle clickers. There's
no clean, deep, **text-and-numbers** sim where the fantasy is *running a real state*:
staring at your nation's vital signs, making a hard call (raise taxes into a slowdown?
cut the fuel subsidy that's bankrupting you but feeds 80M people? pour a decade into
education?), hitting **Advance Year**, and watching the consequences ripple for
decades.

The "whoa" is **emergent statecraft stories**: a fiscal squeeze → austerity → unrest →
a confidence crisis; or a patient 15-year education + R&D bet that finally tips the
economy into a productivity boom. Real countries make the opening position
*recognizable* — playing resource-cursed Nigeria feels nothing like playing
export-engine Germany or debt-laden Japan.

## What makes this cool (delight factors)

- **Real starting positions.** ~16 curated real countries with realistic magnitudes
  (GDP, population, debt, sector mix, government type). The opening *feels* like that
  country's actual predicament.
- **Interlocking systems, not stat bars.** Decisions cascade: tax → deficit → debt →
  credit rating → borrowing cost → budget room → services → approval → stability.
  Pull one lever, feel it everywhere.
- **Ministerial briefings as the UI voice.** Numbers are framed as terse reports from
  your cabinet ("Finance Minister: debt service now eats 14% of revenue, Chancellor.")
  — flavor on top of hard numbers, the delight layer over the spreadsheet.
- **Events with teeth.** Oil shocks, pandemics, elections, droughts, capital flight,
  scandals — branching choices with real tradeoffs, gated on your actual state.
- **Long arcs.** Yearly turns over decades. You author a national trajectory and a
  composite **Statecraft Score** tells the story at the end. **MVP score = prosperity ×
  stability** (each a 0-100 index from tracked state); *legitimacy* and *resilience*
  factors join in the Social/later iterations. Fail states are real: **bankruptcy** and
  **revolution**.

## 范围 / Scope

**MVP (tonight's first playable build) — INCLUDES:**

- Turn loop: yearly turns, start year **2025**, advance → resolve → report.
- **Economy** system: GDP, growth, 3-sector split (agriculture/industry/services),
  unemployment, inflation, productivity.
- **Population & demographics**: total population, growth, labor force, basic age skew,
  education level.
- **Government & politics**: approval rating, political stability, government type,
  (for democracies) election cycles.
- **Fiscal / budget**: tax-rate lever, spending allocation across
  Military / Education / Healthcare / Infrastructure / Welfare / R&D, revenue,
  deficit/surplus, sovereign debt (% GDP), credit rating, debt-service cost.
- **Events engine v1**: weighted random + state-conditional events with branching
  choices (data-driven, easy to author).
- **Decisions per turn**: set tax rate, allocate budget %, enact 0-N available
  policies, resolve any fired events.
- **16 curated real countries** with realistic baseline data (see Country Set).
- **Text/numbers dashboard UI** + ministerial-briefing flavor.
- **Save / load** to `localStorage` (multiple slots), plus new-game country select.
- **Score + fail states** (bankruptcy when debt service + lost market access;
  revolution when stability collapses).
- **Deterministic, seeded RNG** so runs are reproducible and testable.

**Explicitly OUT of MVP (stacked later by the depth-first overnight loop):**

- Diplomacy / international relations / trade / war (needs multi-nation simulation).
- Detailed military combat.
- Technology tree / research projects.
- Natural resources & environment/climate system.
- Historical scenarios & victory conditions beyond sandbox+score.
- Multi-language UI (MVP is **Chinese-first** UI, English-friendly labels; i18n later).

These are not dropped — they are the **roadmap** below, ordered for depth-first stacking.

## 用户故事 / Core loop

1. **New game** → pick a country from the curated list (each shows a 1-line predicament
   and starting vitals).
2. **Dashboard**: see your nation's vital signs grouped by system + cabinet briefings
   flagging what needs attention.
3. **Make decisions** for the year: adjust tax rate, reallocate the budget, enact
   available policies, answer any event prompts.
4. **Advance Year** → engine resolves every system in dependency order, fires events,
   updates the score.
5. **Year report**: what changed and why ("GDP +2.1%: industry rebounded; deficit
   widened to 4.3% of GDP; approval −6 after the fuel-subsidy cut").
6. Repeat across decades. End on a fail state, a chosen end-year, or open-ended sandbox.

## 行为细节 / Key mechanics & edge cases

- **Resolution order matters** (locked in eng plan): demographics → economy →
  fiscal/budget → social effects → politics/approval → stability → events → score.
  Each system is a pure `step(state) → state` reducer.
- **Debt spiral**: high debt → lower credit rating → higher interest → bigger deficit →
  more debt. Loss of market access (rating floor + deficit) → **bankruptcy** fail state.
- **Legitimacy/unrest**: sustained low approval + high unemployment → rising unrest →
  **revolution** fail state (authoritarian states resist longer but fall harder). *MVP
  uses approval + unemployment + stability only; inequality (Gini) deepens this in the
  Social iteration.*
- **Government type modifies levers**: democracies face elections (approval → turnover
  risk) but borrow cheaply; authoritarian states ignore elections but pay a legitimacy
  tax and risk sharper collapse.
- **Numbers stay realistic**: growth, inflation, unemployment clamp to plausible bands;
  no negative populations; budget percentages always normalize to 100%.
- **Determinism**: a seed + the player's choices fully determine the run (testable).

## 数据 / Country set & data grounding

Curated for **gameplay variety** across size, development, government type, and resource
profile. **MVP ships 6 maximally-contrasting countries** (proves the variety thesis
without a 320-number data-authoring tarpit); the loop's first iteration expands to the
full ~16.

**MVP-6** (covers democracy / monarchy / authoritarian / hybrid; developed / developing;
industrial / resource / services; huge / tiny):

> 🇩🇪 Germany (developed democracy, export engine, low debt, aging) ·
> 🇯🇵 Japan (developed democracy, ~250%-of-GDP debt, aging hard) ·
> 🇳🇬 Nigeria (developing, very young, oil-dependent, fragile institutions) ·
> 🇸🇬 Singapore (tiny ultra-rich city-state, trade hub, dominant-party) ·
> 🇸🇦 Saudi Arabia (oil monarchy, sovereign wealth, undiversified) ·
> 🇨🇳 China (authoritarian giant, manufacturing, demographic cliff ahead)

**✅ Now implemented — 16 total** (added post-overnight): 🇺🇸 USA · 🇮🇳 India · 🇬🇧 UK ·
🇫🇷 France · 🇧🇷 Brazil · 🇷🇺 Russia · 🇰🇷 South Korea · 🇿🇦 South Africa · 🇮🇩 Indonesia · 🇲🇽 Mexico

**Reality-grounding policy (per global CLAUDE.md):** starting figures are *approximate
real-world magnitudes* at a stated **baseline ≈ 2024**, used for gameplay — real
relationships (China's GDP ≈ \$18T and population ≈ 1.41B; Japan's debt ≈ 250% of GDP;
Saudi reliance on oil) are preserved, not fabricated to look precise. The data file
**labels every figure as approximate + baseline year**. A checklist item tracks
verifying/refining specific figures against public sources (World Bank, IMF WEO, SIPRI)
during the loop — we don't pretend the numbers are exact, and we don't invent
fake-precise ones.

## 架构 / Recommended approach (detail locked in eng plan)

**Pure engine + thin UI**, vanilla **TypeScript + Vite**, no backend, no UI framework:

- `src/engine/` — framework-free simulation. Single `GameState`; each system is a
  `step(state, ctx) → state` reducer; `advanceTurn` composes them in fixed order.
  Seeded RNG. 100% unit-testable with Vitest.
- `src/data/` — `countries.ts` (typed, labeled-approximate data) + `events.ts`
  (data-driven event definitions) + `policies.ts`.
- `src/ui/` — DOM rendering. Re-render the dashboard on each turn (turns are discrete,
  user-triggered → full re-render is plenty fast, no reactive framework needed).
- `src/main.ts` — wiring, save/load, country select.

Why vanilla: tiny dependency surface, the engine stays portable and trivially testable,
and adding a new system = add a reducer + a panel. Perfect for depth-first stacking.

## Approaches considered (Phase 4)

**APPROACH A — Minimal (rejected).** One country, economy-only, sandbox. Effort S,
Risk Low. Ships in an hour but it's a calculator, not a game. No emergent stories.

**APPROACH B — Modular reducer engine + curated real countries (RECOMMENDED).**
Effort M, Risk Low-Med. MVP = economy+population+government+fiscal+events; clean
`step()` reducers make the overnight depth-first stacking near-free. Testable,
portable, extensible. This is the spine.

**APPROACH C — "Cabinet" narrative framing (folded into B).** Effort M-L, Risk Med.
Pure briefing/narrative front-end over the sim. Too content-heavy as the *whole* game,
but its best idea — **ministerial briefings as the UI voice** — is grafted onto B as
the delight layer.

**Recommendation: B, with C's ministerial-briefing framing.** Minimal viable is too
thin to be fun; B gives a real game tonight and an architecture built for the loop.

## Premises (Phase 3 — challenged & held)

1. **Pure text+numbers can be genuinely fun** if systems interlock and events create
   stories. (Held — Democracy 3/4, Suzerain, Universal Paperclips prove it.)
2. **Real countries beat fictional** for the "real decisions" fantasy, and approximate
   real magnitudes are honest + sufficient. (Held — user chose this; grounding policy
   above keeps it truthful.)
3. **A pure reducer engine is the right backbone** for an overnight depth-first build.
   (Held — it makes "add a system" a localized, testable change.)
4. **Yearly turns over decades** is the right cadence for *national* decisions (budgets
   are annual). (Held.)

## Depth-first roadmap (overnight loop order)

Each iteration = add one dimension end-to-end (engine reducer + data + UI panel +
tests + commit), depth-first:

1. ✅ MVP core: economy + population + government + fiscal + events + score + save
   (6 countries). **First loop task: expand country set 6 → ~16.**
2. **Social system**: healthcare/education outcomes, inequality (Gini), quality of
   life, unrest dynamics feeding stability.
3. **Technology & research**: R&D spend → tech level → sector productivity multipliers;
   research projects.
4. **Military**: force size, readiness, doctrine; military spend tradeoffs; coup risk.
5. **Diplomacy & trade**: other nations as lightweight actors; relations, trade
   balance, alliances, sanctions.
6. **War**: conflict resolution model built on military + diplomacy.
7. **Resources & environment**: energy, key commodities, emissions/climate, depletion.
8. **Events expansion**: large authored event/crisis library + multi-year event chains.
9. **Scenarios & victory**: historical start scenarios, victory conditions, leaderboards.
10. Polish passes interleaved (balance, UX, accessibility) as systems land.

## 验收标准 / Success criteria (MVP)

- [ ] Can start a new game as any available country (6 at MVP) and see realistic starting vitals.
- [ ] Can adjust tax + budget allocation and enact a policy in a turn.
- [ ] Advancing a year visibly changes interlocked stats with an explained year-report.
- [ ] At least one event can fire and its choice measurably changes the trajectory.
- [ ] Bankruptcy and revolution fail states can be reached by bad play.
- [ ] Save → reload restores an identical game (deterministic).
- [ ] Engine has unit tests; runs are reproducible from a seed.
- [ ] `npm run dev` opens a playable game; `npm run build` produces a static bundle.

## 评审修正 / Review fixes incorporated (adversarial spec review, 2026-06-19)

Independent reviewer scored the vision 8/10 but the *implementation-readiness* 4/10:
the numeric model was missing. Resolved as follows:

- **Numeric model → [design-engine.md](design-engine.md).** Full `GameState` schema
  (every field + unit + range), v0 reducer formulas, `StepContext` / `PendingDecisions`
  contracts, seeded-PRNG choice with RNG state serialized into `GameState` (so the
  "identical reload" test passes by construction), credit-rating scale + interest curve,
  bankruptcy/revolution predicates, score formula, and `Policy` / `EventDef` types with
  worked examples. **That doc is the build contract; this spec is the product vision.**
- **Scope cut**: MVP 16 → **6** maximally-contrasting countries (avoids the data tarpit);
  expand to ~16 as the first loop task.
- **Consistency leaks fixed**: MVP revolution predicate uses only MVP stats
  (approval + unemployment + stability), inequality deferred to Social; MVP score is
  2-factor (prosperity × stability), legitimacy/resilience deferred. Education = static
  level in MVP (dynamic *outcomes* deferred); productivity near-static in MVP.
- **Baseline reconciled**: ~2024 approximate figures seed the **2025** starting state.
